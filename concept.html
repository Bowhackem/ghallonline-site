{ origins }

Why I build

I learn how things work. Then I build things. That's been true since long before it was a career — taking apart what's in front of me, whether it's a Linux box, a circuit, or a spreadsheet, until I understand the system underneath it.

I work across software, automation, and AI, and just as seriously across robotics, electronics, and physics — not as separate hobbies, but as the same instinct pointed in different directions.

This site is part of that practice too. It's not finished, and it's not meant to be.

{ how i think }

A few working principles

Understand before you trust

I don't take a system's word for how it works. I open it up first.

Build the smallest true version first

A rough thing that actually works teaches you more than a polished plan that doesn't exist yet.

Stay a beginner somewhere

I keep at least one discipline where I'm still clearly learning. It keeps the rest honest.
